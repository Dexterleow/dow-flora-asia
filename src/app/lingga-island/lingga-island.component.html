Lingga History

The Ancient Lingga Sultanate was situated within the Riau Islands of Indonesia. And, in Ancient Chinese History, it was called the Long Ya Gu Guo (龙牙古国) because the tallest peak of the Lingga Mountain look like the 3-teeth of a dragon.

Lingga Island had been a calling point of silk road by the seas. In Ming Dynasty, Admiral Cheng He had called at Lingga and had established contact with the Sultans. He had called at Linnga and had established contact with Sultans. A war between Lingga and the Northen Sumatra Sultanate had broke out. Lingga Sultan's Palace was burnt down by Banda Aceh Navy and destructed subsequently.

The Lingga Sultan and his followers fled to Singapore Island and finally settled down at Johor Lama.

The Sultan of Johor was told related and originated from Linnga Islands.

Lingga Jetty & Transport

Ferry services to the islands from outside the archipelago come from the provincial capital to the north, Tanjung Pinang on Bintan, including from Singapore. These days the main industry is fishing. There are a number of fine beaches with some coral around the Archipelago but there is very little tourism on account of the poor transport links with the outside world.

  • Singkep has two ports, Dabo near Dabosingkep and Jago near Sungaibuluh. Service to the port of Muntok on P. Bangka of Sumatera Selatan ceased operating regularly with the demise of the tin mining industry. However, a high-speed ferry continues to connect Tanjung Pinang to Singkep, from where local boats may be chartered to Lingga.
  • For Lingga, Daik is the major town and port. It can be reached in a day from Singapore transferring at Tanjung Pinang.
Lingga Mountain

Mount Lingga, Lingga Island

At 1163 meters this is the highest mountain in the Riau Islands.

It is an ancient volcanic plug that rises spectacularly from the surrounding area. The summit consists of three separate pillars with two hundred meter high vertical walls. Pristine untouched wild rain forest surrounds the mountain. Wild, virtually unreachable one to two hundred meter high waterfalls tumble down on several sides of the mountain. According to the local people no one has ever succeeded in climbing the summit.

Lingga Beach

Due to the low number of tourists, Lingga Island's beaches are un-crowded and are great for swimming or snorkeling.

Pristine, untouched and remote beaches surround the coast of Lingga Island, and are great for exploring by travelers.
